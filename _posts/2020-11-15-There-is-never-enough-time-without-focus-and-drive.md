A lot of wannabe developers may think that getting that developer job is the ultimate goal and then they'll move on from there. This post will be about the "there" and that it is as tough to make time to do your stuff while you are doing your job. A developer job is just like any other jobs as you have to focus on the work and technology that your company is using. 

For example, my android developer job focuses a lot on connecting database to the android application and displaying the results on the app. There may be a few calculation but nothing complex. Also, they are moving towards hybrid solution. 

Meanwhile, my goal is always to create something of my own and hopefully can make a business out of it. So I'm focusing more on end-users and mobile-focus stuff that evolves around vibrations, audio, bluetooth, etc. These are not what is implemented at my job so I have study them on my own time.  

Below are what I feel is the common knowledge:
- IDE i.e. android studio, visual studio code
- android sdk, activity lifecycles
- basic programming principles

So in order to achieve my goals, I need to instill that drive to keep myself going while I have to study the company tech stack and also improve myself so that I'm a better android developer. The current company has no testing in place, still use java and has no code review. So I need to learn kotlin and to implement testing. Testing is a must so that you can complete the take-home test for a good enterprise job.

So skill qualification for enterprise jobs may be vastly different from skills that you need to have to make stuff for your end-users. One still need to work very hard to push oneself to produce one's creation.

Nonetheless, one only lives once and to have no regrets one should try one's best to achieve one's desire. It's the journey of struggles and strives that makes life interesting and for me, I always learnt best from my mistakes. My personal motto is "the only failure is failing to try."

So folks, do not let yourself stop you from doing what you want. We can achieve our visions as long as we are willing to do whatever it takes.
