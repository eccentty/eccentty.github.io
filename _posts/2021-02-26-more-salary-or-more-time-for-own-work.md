I've been working as an android development for about a year and I've come to realise that the skills to develop your own business is quite different from that of a corporate developer.

## Business owners
For a business owner, you would do whatever you can to ship your product(s) as fast as possible so that you can get feedback from your customers. Then you will need to promote and increase exposure to your site(s)/app(s). Only when the site/apps gain traction then scalability will come into consideration.


## Corporate developer
On the other hand, a junior corporate developer will need to consider future employability and so will need to constantly update his/her current the tech stacks. The junior also needs to prepare for interviews which most of the time consist of theories and sometimes take-home/on-site assignment (which are not good ideas because these assignments take time). A senior can probably be more choosy and stop the application process then and there. 

I guess it may depend on the developer's life goals. Starting a business is not easy and finding a better paying jobs are faster so many developers follow this route. Nonetheless, there are some exceptional developers who can master both, start a business while looking for a better paying jobs. But not everyone can do this, putting efforts into both ventures. 

## Decision
I changed career from finance to IT, so that I can learn the skills that I can use to start my business while earning something instead of nothing while trying to come up and build the business ideas. A year later, with entry level salary, the tech stack for mobile development is changing and will continue to change a lot. Last few weeks, I've been thinking that it may be time to move on to another company since the company I'm with is developing android apps with java and laravel with MVC for a better salary of course. 

However, I'm starting to think that this idea is flawed. At the current company, with low salary, I have time on my hand and those are the times that I can work on my business ideas. If I were to continue to climb the better salary wagon, it may be an endless struggles. Higher salary also come with higher expectations and more pressure to perform at work. These are not my goals. Many developers advise that a developer should have a wide range of skills and command of many languages, which in the employable sense, those are absolutely a must. 

I guess this post is a public accountability that I'm going to focus on building my business ideas from now on and not focus on looking for better paying jobs. Of course, I've been learning kotlin, flutter, MVVM design patterns, automated testing, CI/CD to be a better developer but I'm no expert at them yet. I hope to use them to build on my business ideas though. 

This is my take on my life journey and I will keep you posted. Meanwhile, if you have time and want to share your thoughts and wisdom, please leave them in the comments.
