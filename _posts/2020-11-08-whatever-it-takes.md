Mobile development started to take off probably in 2009 and it's now about 11 years and it has plateaued in the eyes of consumers. No, I did not fall in love with mobile development but I want to make applications to my own specification. I was working in other fields and I've been trying to learn how to code on-and-off for a few years with no success. Well, a lot of people will say try harder.

Nonetheless, in 2018, I've decided that if I'm going to immerse myself in this application building world, I might as well try to get a job in the industry. So what's stopping me? Mainly self-confidence and my lack of skills. So I asked 2 people that I know who are in the field (a 2-year programmer turned project manager with 10+ years of experience and an IT business owner) whether they still value a degree. According to them, they're be more lenient towards people with degrees. If 2 candidates were given the same test, the one without a degree have to perform with flying colors to be accepted.

Given my lack of confidence and my past learning failures, I've decided to enrolled into a Master Degree in CS at a local university. I need to pass the prerequisite tests which include data structures, basic programming language and discrete maths. With support of my family (for daily living) and enough fund to pay for the course (my saving), I quitted my job and studied the prerequisites to getting into the university. Fast forward 2 years later, I completed the degree but I still owned my supervisor certain parts of my would-be journal (not something I want to publish as it was not required by my degree but my supervisor needs the journal publication for her tenure/promotion proposal). 

I made an android application for my thesis but the application consisted of a few GOD activities with no software design, no database connection, no networking, no json. With the application, my "recent" degree and my entry-level salary demand help to make my resume stand out so I'm getting interviews BUT the applciation assignments were ON ANOTHER LEVEL. The market-ready-level. 

In 2019, I was still using findViewById and that was a hugh mistake. I was focusing on getting the application out so I can finish my degree and not learning the android development. In one of the interviews in early 2020, I was asked whether I know "view binding" or "data binding" or RX. In short, I was not prepared for android developer interviews. I applied so I can learn what is in the market and what kind of knowledge are needed. The interviews were definitely eye-openers.

For those are interested enough to read this through, thank you ^^.
