I'm almost 9 months into my first android developer job and I'm still searching for forums on career transition and such. Such questions are asked again and again by people of all ages and even me who are already in the career itself. Maybe it's a habit of sort, a kind of procrastination technique that we all used. With so many questions and answers out there, we should already know what to do.

Nonetheless, I'll put forth my take on the subject of career change into application development. My brief background can be found [here](https://eccentty.github.io/whatever-it-take/).

First, you have to determine your time, financial resources, support and your goals.
Then, you have to be prepare to work for it, do whatever it take.

If you think, you need a degree and you have time and money to do it, then you can go and get it but remember that it'll only be good to pass the HRs' tickboxes and only valuable for the first few years.

## If your goal is to be employable:
No matter what options you chose, the ultimate result will be whether you can do the given assignments. Your personal projects (not coding assignments but working application(s)) are stuff you can talk about during your interviews, good to have and helps to make your resume stand out.

For android development, the take home assignments that I have came across previously were:

### A 2-hour android app (on-site, internet allowed):
- list a few train stations 
- clicking on each of the train station will show you a list of nearby hospitals with calculated distance from the given train station to the respective hospitals
- Requires networking library


### Country list app (1 week take-home assignment, instruction said it should take about 2-3 hours):
- Require networking library
- display list of country and flags 
- clicking on country will display countries, flags, list of langauges and telephone prefixes.
- Clicking on phone prefixes will bring the user to the phone app that display the prefix number for further input
- have bookmark function
- if have testing is a plus


### News app (1 week take-home assignment, instruction said it should not take too long):
- Require networking library
- The app comes with 50% functions implemented but the image display function needs to be corrected
- redesign the architecture of the app
- to include immersive feature for tablet
- to include testing


### An android app that should follow a given design (1-2 days take-home assignment)
- pass data to and forth the fragment-activity, activty-activity, web-app
- bottom navigation design


For wanna-be-application-developers, if you want to work at a good company, you should aim to pass the test(s) that require testing. Those stringent tests mean that these standards are mostly applied in their working environment. 

I'm writing this because, I did not pass these tests and ended up with a company that was easy to enter i.e. with just an interview. Due to the lack of required skills, I only asked for entry-level wage and was accepted. I'll post the frustration that I'm dealing with in another post.

So if you can create the above applications, you are ready for the market.
